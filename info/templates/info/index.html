Explanation

Coronary heart disease (CHD), also known as coronary artery diesease (CAD) is the most common type of heart disease. The disease develops as a result of atherosclerosis, the buildup of fats, cholesterol and other substances in and on your artery walls (plaque), which can restrict blood flow. The plaque can burst, triggering a blood clot. It is when atherosclerosis occurs in the coronary arteries, the blood vessels that transport oxygen and blood to the heart, that CHD occurs.In some cases, this can lead to cases of heart attack. The disease's symptoms develop gradually, making it more common among the elderly.

Symptoms

  • Chest Pain (Angina)  - discomfort caused when your heart muscle doesn't get enough oxygen-rich blood. It may feel like pressure or squeezing in your chest. The discomfort also can occur in your shoulders, arms, neck, jaw, or back.
  • Shortness Of Breath  - If your heart can't pump enough blood to meet your body's needs, you may develop either shortness of breath or extreme fatigue with exertion.
  • Dizziness
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Sweating
  • Increased Heart Rate
  • Heartburn
  • Upper Body Pain
  • Discomfort In The Arms, Back, Neck, Jaw, or Upper Stomach.

Risk Factors

  • Hypertension
  • Raised Serum Lipids
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Diets High in Saturated Fats and Cholesterol
  • Physical Inactivity
  • Obesity

Statistics

  • Among the American population above 18 years of age, about 1 out of every 13 people is affected by CHD.
  • It is most prevalent among Asian Americans, Pacific Islanders, American Indians, and Alaska Natives.
  • CHD takes the lives of over 370,000 people every year.
  • By 2010 CHD is estimated to be the leading cause of death in developing countries
